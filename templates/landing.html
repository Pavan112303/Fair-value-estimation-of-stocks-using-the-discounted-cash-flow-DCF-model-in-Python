How DCF Valuation Works

Understanding the Discounted Cash Flow model

Project Future Cash Flows

Estimate the company's free cash flows for the next 5-10 years based on growth assumptions.

Calculate Terminal Value

Estimate the company's value beyond the projection period using the Gordon Growth Model.

Determine WACC

Calculate the Weighted Average Cost of Capital using risk-free rate, beta, and market risk premium.

Discount to Present Value

Apply the discount rate to bring all future cash flows to their present value.

Calculate Fair Value

Sum of present values gives enterprise value. Subtract net debt and divide by shares to get fair value per share.

About DCF Valuation

The Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) model is a fundamental valuation method used to estimate the intrinsic value of a stock.

Key Assumptions:
  • Growth Rates: Expected annual growth in free cash flows
  • WACC: Weighted Average Cost of Capital (risk-free rate + beta × market risk premium)
  • Terminal Growth: Long-term growth rate (typically 2-3%)
